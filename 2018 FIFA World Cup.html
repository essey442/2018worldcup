    • The 2018 FIFA world cup was an international football tournament contested by men's national teams that took place between 14 June and 15 July 2018 in Russia. It was the 21st FIFA world cup, a worldwide football tournament held once every four years.It was the eleventh time the championships had been held in Europe, and the first time they were held in Eastern Europe. At an estimated cost of over $14.2 billion, it was the most expensive world cup to date.

    The tournament phase involved 32 teams, of which 31 came through qualifying competitions, while the host nation Russia was qualified automatically. Host nation Russia was eliminated in the quarter-finals. In the final, France played Croatia on 15 July at the Luzhniki stadium in Moscow. France won the match 4-2 to claim thier second World Cup. Croatian player Luka Modric was voted the tournament's best player, winning the Golden Ball. England's Harry Kane scored the most goals during the tournament with six. Belgian Thibaut Courtois won the Golden Glove, awarded for the best goalkeeper of the tournament. And Kylian Mbappe was awarded the best young player of the tournament.

    Knockout stage

    In the knockout stages, if a match was level at the end of normal playing time, extra time was played (two periods of 15 minutes each) and followed, if necessary, by a penality shoot-out to determine the winners.
